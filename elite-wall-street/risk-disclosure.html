Leverage Risk

Leverage allows you to control larger positions with a smaller amount of capital. While leverage can amplify profits, it can also magnify losses.

  • High Leverage = High Risk: A small market movement can result in significant gains or losses
  • Margin Calls: If the market moves against you, you may be required to deposit additional funds
  • Liquidation: Your positions may be closed automatically if you don't have sufficient margin
  • Losses Can Exceed Deposits: You could lose more than your initial investment

Example: With 100:1 leverage, a 1% market move can result in a 100% gain OR a 100% loss of your margin.

Market Volatility

Financial markets are subject to extreme volatility which can cause rapid price changes:

  • Economic Events: News releases, interest rate decisions, and economic data can cause sudden price movements
  • Geopolitical Events: Political instability, elections, and conflicts can impact markets
  • Market Gaps: Prices can "gap" over weekends or during major events, potentially triggering stop losses at worse prices
  • Liquidity Risk: During volatile periods, it may be difficult to execute trades at desired prices
  • Slippage: Your order may be filled at a different price than expected

Cryptocurrency Risks

Cryptocurrencies are extremely volatile and carry additional risks:

  • Extreme Volatility: Prices can swing 20-50% or more in a single day
  • Regulatory Risk: Governments may ban or restrict cryptocurrency trading
  • Technology Risk: Blockchain networks can experience technical issues
  • Security Risk: Exchanges and wallets can be hacked
  • No Intrinsic Value: Cryptocurrencies have no underlying asset backing
  • Market Manipulation: Crypto markets are less regulated and prone to manipulation

Warning: You could lose your entire cryptocurrency investment!
